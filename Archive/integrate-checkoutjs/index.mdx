\_window.CKOConfig\_ is the recommended and safest way to configure Checkout.js. Run it both synchronously or asynchronously — \_it's up to you\_. This simple integration method provides a widget displaying \*\*accepted payment icons\*\* alongside a \*\*Pay Now\*\* button that triggers the \*\*payment form\*\*. After completing the form, Checkout.com passes a card token to your server-side code for use. Look at the window.CKOConfig demo below and click the \*\*Pay Now\*\* button to launch the payment form. Fill the fields in like this: \* email address — we have completed this for you \* use one of our \[test cards\](doc:testing#section-test-card-numbers), for example, :fa-cc-visa: <span class="card-number">4242 4242 4242 4242</span> \* use any future expiry date (mm/yy) \* and the CVV is :fa-lock: \*\*100\*\*

Checkout.configure() is a variant of our Checkout.js integration method. It allows the payment Lightbox to be opened directly without the \*\*Pay Now\*\* button. This flexibility enables you to use a personalized payment button, or to open the payment Lightbox instantaneously. 

Checkout.js can only be run synchronously when using Checkout.configure(). 

\## Embedding Checkout.configure() Checkout.configure() can be set up with two different variants, as such, we have two different configuration examples to try: \* \[Example A :fa-code:\](doc:integrate-checkoutjs#section-example-a-open-the-payment-lightbox-instantly) opens the payment Lightbox instantly, without the use of a Pay Now button. \* \[Example B :fa-code:\](doc:integrate-checkoutjs#section-example-b-use-a-custom-pay-now-button) uses a custom Pay Now button to launch the payment Lightbox. For both examples, place the provided code snippet :fa-code: on your page. You will need to replace the \`publicKey\` with your own and configure some extra attributes. You can find out about these and what they do in the \[JavaScript keys table\](doc:integrate-checkoutjs#section-javascript-keys). ### <span class="example\_hl">Example A:</span> Open the payment Lightbox instantly In this example, you can see a demonstration of Checkout.configure() instantly generating the payment Lightbox. On the mock webpage, the link takes the user to a new page where the payment Lightbox opens up immediately. For this, you can use a text link, a button, or even an image to link to the payment form. As such, Checkout.configure() is an extremely versatile method of integrating Checkout.js. Checkout.render() renders the payment widget automatically onto your page. It displays both the accepted payment icons, the Pay Now button, and the Lightbox payment form. Once your customer completes the payment form, the card details are securely sent to us and exchanged for a card token. 

Checkout.render() offers an asynchronous payment process. This means that it can take several days to confirm the success or failure of a payment. 

\## Embedding Checkout.render() Use the code snippet :fa-code: below to fit Checkout.render() into your page. To start collecting tokens, first, swap the \`publicKey\` for your own, and then configure the additional \[JavaScript keys\](doc:integrate-checkoutjs#section-javascript-keys).
